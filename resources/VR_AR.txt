Virtual Reality (VR) — Report

1) What is Virtual Reality (VR)?

Virtual Reality (VR) is a computer-generated, immersive environment that
completely replaces the user’s view of the real world with a simulated
3D environment. Users typically access VR through head-mounted displays
(HMDs) and input devices (controllers, gloves, trackers). VR systems
combine stereoscopic displays, head-tracking, spatial audio, and often
handheld controllers or body sensors to create presence — the feeling of
“being there.”
Source: https://splunk.com/en_us/data-insider/what-is-vr.html

Use-cases: Entertainment & gaming, training & simulation, education,
therapy.
Source:
https://www.startus-insights.com/innovators-guide/virtual-reality-trends/

------------------------------------------------------------------------

2) VR vs AR — the core differences

-   VR: Fully replaces the real world with a virtual environment
    (isolation).
-   AR: Overlays digital content on the real world.
    Source: https://www.shopify.com/blog/ar-vr

Hardware:
- VR: HMDs like Meta Quest, HTC Vive, Valve Index
- AR: Smartphones, tablets, smart glasses (HoloLens, Magic Leap)

Interaction:
- VR: High immersion (3D audio, controllers, haptics)
- AR: Contextual overlay (navigation, guidance)

Use-cases:
- VR: Training, gaming, simulations
- AR: Retail try-on, procedural guidance, remote assistance

3) Current trends in VR/XR

1.  Hardware: Standalone headsets like Meta Quest evolving.
2.  Standards: OpenXR and WebXR adoption.
3.  Haptics: Gloves, vests.
4.  AI convergence: Procedural worlds, AI NPCs, asset generation.
5.  Enterprise adoption: Digital twins, training, XR collaboration.
6.  Cloud streaming: Remote GPU rendering.
7.  Safety/privacy: Regulatory focus on social VR safety.
8.  Metaverse/social XR: Shifting from consumer hype to enterprise
    collaboration.

4) Technology stack

Hardware: HMDs (Meta Quest, HTC Vive, Pico, HoloLens).
APIs: OpenXR, WebXR.
Engines: Unity, Unreal, Three.js, Babylon.js, A-Frame.
3D Assets: Blender, Maya, 3ds Max, Substance; glTF format.
Graphics APIs: Vulkan, DirectX, Metal.
Networking: WebRTC, cloud GPU streaming.
Audio: FMOD, Wwise, spatial audio SDKs.
Computer Vision: ARKit, ARCore, SLAM libraries.

Sources:
- https://www.khronos.org/openxr/
- https://developer.oculus.com/documentation/unity/unity-xr/
- https://aframe.io/

7) Opportunities & challenges

Opportunities: Enterprise training, device-agnostic solutions, consumer
adoption via affordable headsets.
Challenges: Device fragmentation, costly 3D asset pipelines, UX/motion
sickness, social VR safety/privacy.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

8) Recommendations

-   Use Unity or Unreal with OpenXR.
-   Prioritize performance (90+ FPS, latency control).
-   Adopt glTF asset pipelines.
-   Build for enterprise first (ROI-focused).
-   Implement moderation & privacy if building social XR.
